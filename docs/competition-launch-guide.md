## What Is EliteRank?

EliteRank is a competition platform that lets organizations run nomination-based contests where contestants are nominated (or self-enter), the public votes for their favorites, and winners are crowned. Think of it as a modern, gamified version of "Most Eligible" or "Best Of" competitions — complete with paid voting, bonus vote challenges, sponsor integrations, and real-time leaderboards.

---

## Your Role as Host

As a competition host, you are the face of the competition in your city. You have access to a **Host Dashboard** where you can:

- Monitor entries, votes, and revenue in real time
- Approve or reject nominees
- Manage contestants, judges, and sponsors
- Create announcements, events, and bonus vote tasks
- Define prizes and competition rules
- Track your payout earnings

You do **not** need to handle the technical setup — the EliteRank admin team creates the competition shell (city, category, demographic, dates). Your job is to manage the people, content, and promotion.

## Before You Launch

### 1. Confirm Your Competition Details

Work with the EliteRank admin team to finalize:

- **City** — Where is this competition based?
- **Category** — What type of competition? (e.g., Most Eligible)
- **Demographic** — Who is eligible? (e.g., Women 21–39, Men 25–45)
- **Season/Year** — When does it run?
- **Number of Winners** — How many winners will be crowned? (typically 5)
- **Eligibility Radius** — How close to the city must contestants live? Options: must be in city, 10, 25, 50, or 100 miles

### 2. Decide on Entry Type

There are three ways contestants can enter:

| Entry Type | Description |
|---|---|
| **Nominations** | Third parties nominate someone they know (most common) |
| **Applications** | People enter themselves directly |
| **Appointments** | You (the host) manually add contestants |

Most competitions use **nominations** — it creates buzz because people are excited to tell someone "I nominated you!"

### 3. Choose Selection Criteria

- **Votes Only** — Winners are determined purely by public vote count
- **Hybrid (Votes + Judges)** — Public votes are combined with judge scores for final ranking. Use this if you want a quality filter beyond popularity

### 4. Set Your Timeline

Your competition follows a structured timeline with these key phases:

| Phase | What Happens |
|---|---|
| **Coming Soon** | Competition is published but not yet accepting entries. Builds anticipation. Interest forms are open for potential hosts, sponsors, judges, and fans |
| **Nominations Open** | People can nominate contestants (or contestants self-enter). You review and approve entries |
| **Voting Rounds** | The public votes for their favorite contestants. You can have multiple rounds where contestants are eliminated |
| **Judging Rounds** (optional) | If using hybrid selection, judges score contestants during dedicated judging windows |
| **Finals** | Winners are announced and celebrated |

You can have **multiple nomination periods** and **multiple voting rounds** (e.g., Round 1 → Resurrection Round → Finals). Work with the admin team to set the exact dates.

**Important timing rules:**
- Nomination end must be after nomination start
- Voting cannot start before nominations start (but they can overlap)
- Finals date must be after the last voting round ends

## Setting Up Your Competition

Once the admin team creates your competition, you'll have access to the **Host Dashboard**. Here's what to configure:

### About Section

This is the public-facing description of your competition:

- **Tagline** — A short, catchy one-liner (e.g., "Find the Most Eligible in Chicago")
- **Description** — A paragraph explaining what the competition is about
- **Traits** — Key qualities you're looking for in contestants
- **Age Range** — Display text for eligible age range
- **Requirements** — Any special eligibility requirements

### Rules

Organize your competition rules into sections. Be clear about:

- Who can enter (age, location, relationship status, etc.)
- What's expected of contestants during the competition
- How voting works
- How winners are determined
- Code of conduct

### Prizes

Define what winners receive:

- Prize descriptions (e.g., "Feature in City Magazine," "VIP Event Access")
- Prize images
- Cash prizes or gift values (if applicable)

### Sponsors

Add your sponsors with:

- Sponsor name and logo
- Tier level: **Platinum**, **Gold**, or **Silver**
- Website link
- Contribution amount (for internal tracking)

### Judges (if using Hybrid selection)

Add each judge with:

- Name and title
- Bio/credentials
- Photo
- Display order

## Managing Nominations & Entries

This is one of your most important responsibilities as host.

### How Nominations Work

1. A **nominator** visits the competition page and nominates someone
2. They provide: nominee's name, email/phone, age, location, Instagram, a short bio, and why they're nominating them
3. The nominee receives an **invitation** (email + push notification) with a unique claim link
4. The nominee clicks the link and either **accepts** or **declines**
5. If accepted, they complete their profile (photo, bio, details) and become a **contestant**

### Nominee Statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Pending** | Nomination received, awaiting your review |
| **Approved** | You've approved them; invitation sent to nominee |
| **Converted** | Nominee accepted and completed their profile — now a contestant |
| **Declined** | Nominee declined the invitation |
| **Rejected** | You rejected the nomination |
| **Expired** | Nominee never responded to the invitation |

### What You Should Do

- **Review pending nominations regularly** — Don't let them pile up. Quick approvals keep momentum going
- **Resend invitations** if nominees haven't responded after a few days
- **Reject nominations** that clearly don't meet eligibility criteria
- **Monitor your contestant count** — You need a minimum number of contestants (typically 40) for a healthy competition. If entries are slow, promote harder or extend the nomination period

### Self-Entry Flow

If your competition allows self-entry, people can enter themselves. The flow is:

1. Choose "I'm entering myself"
2. Answer eligibility questions
3. Upload a photo
4. Fill in profile details (name, age, location, Instagram)
5. Write a self-pitch / bio
6. Animated card reveal confirming their entry

Self-entrants are automatically approved (no need for you to review).

## Running Voting Rounds

### How Voting Works

- Every user gets **1 free vote per day** per competition
- Users can purchase **additional votes** at your set price (default $1.00/vote)
- Bulk discounts are available if price bundling is enabled:

| Votes | Price Per Vote | Discount |
|---|---|---|
| 1–10 | $1.00 | — |
| 11–25 | $0.90 | 10% off |
| 26–50 | $0.85 | 15% off |
| 51–100 | $0.80 | 20% off |
| 101–250 | $0.70 | 30% off |
| 251–500 | $0.50 | 50% off |

### Multiple Rounds

You can structure voting in rounds where contestants are progressively eliminated:

- **Round 1** — All contestants compete; top X advance
- **Resurrection Round** (optional) — Eliminated contestants get a second chance
- **Finals** — Remaining contestants compete for the win

Each round has its own start and end date. Between rounds, there's a "between rounds" state where voting is paused.

### Double Vote Days

Special event days can be marked as **double vote days** where every free vote counts as 2. Great for driving engagement spikes.

## Using Judges (Optional)

If your competition uses **hybrid selection** (votes + judges):

1. Add judges in the **People** tab of your dashboard
2. During a **judging round**, judges submit scores for contestants
3. Final rankings combine public vote totals with judge scores
4. This helps ensure quality winners, not just the most popular

Judging rounds are separate from voting rounds and have their own date windows.

## Managing Your Dashboard

Your Host Dashboard has four main tabs:

### Dashboard (Overview)

At a glance:

- **Current Phase** — Where you are in the competition timeline
- **Revenue** — Total money earned from paid votes
- **Your Payout** — Your earnings (percentage of revenue)
- **Traffic** — Profile views and shares
- **Leaderboard** — Top contestants by vote count
- **Upcoming Events** — What's next on the calendar
- **Timeline** — Visual progress through the competition

### People

Manage all the humans involved:

- **Nominees** — Review, approve, reject, resend invites
- **Contestants** — View active contestants, their votes, rank, and trends
- **Judges** — Add and manage judge profiles
- **Sponsors** — Add and manage sponsor information

### Content

Create engagement content:

- **Announcements** — Post updates and news (can pin important ones)
- **Events** — Add competition events with dates, times, and locations
- **Bonus Votes** — Create tasks contestants can complete for extra votes
- **Video Prompts** — Set video submission challenges for contestants
- **Rules** — Organize competition rules by section
- **Prizes** — Define and display winner prizes

### Setup

Configuration settings:

- Competition details (name, status, limits)
- Timeline management (nomination periods, voting rounds)
- About section editor
- Organization branding (logo, colors)
- Prize pool settings

## Content & Engagement

### Bonus Votes

One of the most powerful engagement tools. Create tasks that contestants complete for extra votes:

**Default task ideas:**
- Follow the competition's social media accounts
- Share the competition with friends
- Post a video response to a prompt
- Attend a competition event

**How it works:**
1. You create a bonus vote task with a vote reward (e.g., +5 votes)
2. Contestants see available tasks on their profile
3. They complete the task and submit proof (photo or link)
4. You review the submission and approve/deny
5. Approved submissions automatically add votes to the contestant

### Announcements

Keep contestants and fans engaged with regular updates:

- Voting round start/end reminders
- Leaderboard shakeups
- Event announcements
- Sponsor spotlights
- Winner teasers

Pin important announcements so they stay at the top.

### Events

Create in-person or virtual events tied to your competition:

- Launch parties
- Meet-and-greets
- Finalist reveal events
- Awards ceremonies

Each event can have a date, time, location, and description.

## Revenue & Payouts

### How Revenue Works

Revenue comes from **paid votes**. When someone buys votes for a contestant:

1. Payment is processed through Stripe
2. The vote is recorded and the contestant's count updates in real time
3. Revenue is tracked on your dashboard

### Your Payout

As host, you receive a percentage of total competition revenue (typically **20%**, but this is configured per competition). You can see your running payout total on the dashboard overview.

### Prize Pool

If a minimum prize pool is configured, a portion of revenue goes toward contestant prizes. This is displayed publicly to incentivize voting.

## Tips for a Successful Competition

### Before Launch

- [ ] Have your "about" section, rules, and prizes finalized before going live
- [ ] Line up at least 2–3 sponsors before the nomination period opens
- [ ] Prepare social media content for launch day
- [ ] Brief your judges (if using hybrid selection) on their role and timeline

### During Nominations

- [ ] Promote heavily in the first 48 hours — momentum matters
- [ ] Approve nominees quickly (within 24 hours) to keep the energy up
- [ ] Aim for at least **40 contestants** before voting opens
- [ ] Encourage nominees to complete their profiles with great photos and bios
- [ ] Use social media to spotlight early entries and create FOMO
- [ ] Resend invitations to nominees who haven't claimed after 3 days

### During Voting

- [ ] Post daily or near-daily announcements to keep engagement high
- [ ] Highlight leaderboard changes ("New #1!" or "Only 5 votes separate the top 3!")
- [ ] Use bonus vote tasks to drive social media engagement
- [ ] Schedule a double vote day mid-round for an engagement spike
- [ ] Remind contestants to share their profile link with friends and family
- [ ] Create events (virtual or in-person) tied to voting milestones

### Finals

- [ ] Build anticipation in the final days ("48 hours left to vote!")
- [ ] Plan a reveal event or announcement for winners
- [ ] Have prizes ready to distribute
- [ ] Thank sponsors, judges, contestants, and voters publicly
- [ ] Collect testimonials from winners for future competition promotion

### General Best Practices

- **Respond quickly** — When nominees, contestants, or voters reach out, fast responses build trust
- **Be fair and transparent** — Clearly communicate rules and stick to them
- **Leverage social proof** — Share contestant count milestones ("100 entries and counting!")
- **Create urgency** — Countdown timers and deadline reminders drive action
- **Celebrate everyone** — Even non-winners had the courage to enter. Acknowledge them
- **Document everything** — Photos, videos, and testimonials from this competition will help you launch the next one

## Quick Reference: Competition Phases

```
DRAFT → COMING SOON → LIVE → COMPLETED → ARCHIVED
                        │
                        ├── Nominations Open
                        ├── Between Rounds
                        ├── Voting Round 1
                        ├── Between Rounds
                        ├── Voting Round 2 / Resurrection
                        ├── Between Rounds
                        ├── Judging (if hybrid)
                        └── Finals
```

Phase transitions happen **automatically** based on the dates configured in your timeline. You don't need to manually switch phases — just make sure your dates are set correctly.
