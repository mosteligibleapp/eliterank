# Most Eligible — Host Onboarding Guide

Welcome to Most Eligible! This guide walks you through everything you need to launch and run a successful competition in your city.

---

## What Is Most Eligible?

Most Eligible is a singles competition platform where cities crown their most dynamic, successful, and eligible singles. Nominees are submitted by the community, contestants compete for votes, and winners are celebrated at a live finale event.

Your job as a host is to recruit nominees, build hype, secure sponsors, and manage the competition from start to finish.

---

## Before You Launch

### What You Need to Prepare

**Competition Details**
- Your city and season year (e.g., "Chicago 2026")
- Competition category (Bachelorettes, Bachelors, etc.)
- Number of winners (typically 5)
- Eligibility radius (how far from the city contestants can live)

**Timeline & Dates**
- Nomination open and close dates
- Voting round dates (Round 1, Round 2, Finals)
- Finale event date (the "Night of Nominees" or similar)

**Judges (3–5 recommended)**
- Full name and title/role
- Headshot photo
- Short bio
- Instagram handle

**Sponsors**
- Company name and tier (Platinum, Gold, Silver)
- Sponsorship amount
- Logo image

**Prizes**
- Prize title and description (e.g., "Diamond Necklace from Kay Jewelers")
- Prize image
- Dollar value
- Sponsor/brand name

**Events**
- Event name, date, time, and location
- Ticket link (if applicable)
- Event image/flyer

**Charity Partner (Optional)**
- Organization name
- Logo
- Website URL

**Content**
- Competition rules (eligibility, voting rules, advancement rules, etc.)
- About section description

## Your Dashboard

Once your competition is created, you'll have access to the Host Dashboard. Here's what each section does:

### Overview Tab
Your command center. See real-time stats at a glance:
- Total nominations and contestant count
- Sponsor revenue
- Top 5 contestants by votes
- Upcoming events
- Recent announcements

### People Tab
Manage your entire roster:

**Nominees** — People who have been nominated (by themselves or others)
- **Approve** nominees to make them official contestants
- **Reject** nominees who don't meet eligibility
- **Resend invite** to nominees who haven't completed their profile
- **Repair accounts** if nominees have login issues

**Contestants** — Approved nominees who appear on the public leaderboard
- View their vote count and ranking
- Download achievement cards for social sharing
- Remove contestants if needed
- Reorder final rankings (for completed competitions)

**Winners** — Assign and order winners after the competition ends

### Setup Tab
Configure everything about your competition:

**Timeline & Status**
- Set nomination periods and voting round dates
- Manage competition phase (Draft → Published → Live → Completed)

**Judges**
- Add judges with photo, title, bio, and Instagram
- Edit or remove judges anytime
- Judges display on the public competition page

**Sponsors**
- Add sponsors with tier level and amount
- Sponsors appear on the public page and contribute to the prize pool

**Events**
- Schedule events with date, time, location, and image
- Events appear on the public timeline
- Add ticket links for easy registration

**Prizes**
- Add winner prizes with images and values
- Prizes display in the "Prize Pool" section on the public page

**Rules**
- Create rule sections (Eligibility, Voting Rules, Advancement, etc.)
- Rules appear in an accordion on the public page

**Bonus Vote Tasks**
- Configure tasks contestants can complete to earn bonus votes
- Default tasks: Complete profile, add photo, add bio, add social links, view "How to Win," share profile
- **Custom tasks with proof**: Contestants submit a link (e.g., Instagram post) and you approve/reject
- **Host-managed tasks** (attendance): You manually confirm who completed it — great for "Attend Night of Nominees" type tasks

**Charity Partner**
- Assign a charity partner with logo and website

### Content Tab
- Edit the About section text
- Manage announcements (create, pin, edit, delete)
- Configure organization branding (logo, website)

## The Competition Lifecycle

### Phase 1: Nominations (2–4 weeks recommended)

**What happens:**
- The public page goes live with a "Nominate" button
- People can nominate themselves or others
- Nominees receive an email/text invitation to claim their spot
- Nominees who accept create a profile (photo, bio, Instagram)

**Your job:**
- Promote the nomination link everywhere — social media, email, word of mouth
- Review incoming nominees daily
- Approve quality nominees quickly (within 24 hours)
- Reject ineligible nominees
- Resend invites to people who haven't completed their profile
- Post announcements to build excitement
- Recruit judges and add them to the page
- Secure sponsors and prizes

**Pro tips:**
- Aim for 40+ contestants before voting starts
- The more nominees, the more engagement during voting
- Share the nomination link in group chats, at events, and on social
- Personal outreach converts better than mass blasts

### Phase 2: Voting (1–3 rounds, 1–2 weeks each)

**What happens:**
- Contestants appear on a public leaderboard ranked by votes
- Anyone can buy votes ($1 per vote by default)
- Contestants earn bonus votes by completing tasks
- Multiple rounds can eliminate lower-ranked contestants

**Your job:**
- Monitor the leaderboard daily
- Review and approve bonus task submissions (proof-based tasks)
- Confirm attendance for host-managed tasks
- Post announcements with updates and milestones
- Promote events and ticket sales
- Engage with contestants — encourage them to share their profiles
- Watch for any issues and use the repair tools if needed

**Pro tips:**
- Pin important announcements (voting deadlines, event details)
- Create urgency — "24 hours left to vote!"
- Spotlight top contestants in announcements
- Host viewing parties or meet-and-greets to drive engagement

### Phase 3: Finals & Results

**What happens:**
- Final voting round determines the winners
- Winners are displayed on the results page
- "Think You Can Win?" CTA links to the next season

**Your job:**
- Assign and order winners in the People tab
- Post a congratulations announcement
- Share winner cards on social media
- Plan the next season!

## Bonus Vote Tasks — How They Work

Bonus votes are free votes contestants earn by completing tasks. This drives engagement and rewards active contestants.

### Default Tasks (Auto-Awarded)
These are checked automatically when a contestant visits the competition page:
- **Complete your profile** — Name, bio, and city filled out
- **Add a profile photo** — Upload an avatar
- **Write your bio** — Add a bio to their profile
- **Add a social link** — Connect Instagram, TikTok, or LinkedIn
- **View How to Win** — Read the competition guide
- **Share your profile** — Share their profile link

### Custom Tasks (Proof-Based)
You create these. Contestants submit a link as proof, and you approve or reject.
- Example: "Post about the competition on Instagram" → Contestant pastes their post link → You review and approve

### Host-Managed Tasks (Attendance)
You create these for in-person events. Contestants can see the task but can't submit anything — you manually confirm who attended.
- Example: "Attend Night of Nominees" → After the event, go to Setup → click "Manage" on the task → confirm each attendee

## Events & Timeline

Events are a huge driver of engagement. Schedule them throughout the competition:

- **Launch Party** — Kick off nominations
- **Night of Nominees** — Meet the contestants (use a host-managed bonus task to track attendance)
- **Viewing Parties** — Watch the leaderboard together
- **Finale Event** — Crown the winners

Each event can have:
- Date, time, and location
- Promotional image
- Ticket link
- Description

Events appear on the public timeline and as "Upcoming Event" cards during voting.

## Common Questions

**Q: How do I get more nominations?**
Personal outreach is king. DM people directly, post in group chats, share at events. The nomination link is your best friend.

**Q: What if someone nominates someone who doesn't want to participate?**
They'll receive an invite but can simply ignore it. You can also reject the nomination from the People tab.

**Q: Can I add contestants manually?**
Yes — use the "+ Add" button in the Contestants section of the People tab.

**Q: How do votes work financially?**
Votes are purchased at the price per vote you set (default $1). Revenue from votes contributes to the prize pool. Stripe handles all payments.

**Q: What happens between voting rounds?**
Lower-ranked contestants may be eliminated (depending on your advancement rules). Remaining contestants move to the next round.

**Q: Can I change dates after launching?**
Yes — update timeline dates anytime from the Setup tab. Changes take effect immediately.

**Q: How do I contact EliteRank support?**
Reach out to the Most Eligible team directly or use the in-app support channels.
